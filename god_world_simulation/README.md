# God World Simulation

In the "god world," every patient has a fixed, true probability of responding to treatment — a latent biological reality that is never directly observable. The "observed world" is what a clinical trial actually measures: noisy binary outcomes drawn from those latent probabilities, filtered through imperfect enrollment criteria, adherence, and follow-up. Because a trial fits a model (e.g., logistic regression) to observed outcomes rather than to ground-truth response probabilities, the estimated Number Needed to Treat (NNT) reflects the noise structure and selection artifacts of the trial design rather than the true causal effect in the population; as a result, the predicted NNT diverges systematically from the true NNT computed directly from the god-world probabilities, and the goal of this simulation is to quantify and understand that divergence under varying assumptions about population heterogeneity, measurement noise, and model misspecification.
